I bought some pork shoulder stew meat and was looking for something different to do with it than my usual italian flavor combos. Claude suggested an estofado which I'd never heard of but this was a total winner.

Followed the recipe pretty closely with a few swaps based on what I had. Swapped the fresh bell pepper for jarred piquillo peppers, which I added in the last 20 minutes so they wouldn't dissolve. Used whole tinned San Marzanos instead of the chopped ones and just smashed them against the sides of the pot as I went. Used yellow potatoes since the recipe didn't specify and claude said I needed something with thin skins & waxy flesh so they'd stay intact in the stew.

The pork came out super tender and flavorful, and the whole thing was incredibly easy to make. A nice change from how we've been eating pork lately.

## Ingredients
- Pork shoulder, 1 lb, cut into 1.5" cubes
- Chorizo, sliced
- Smoked paprika
- Jarred piquillo peppers (in place of fresh bell pepper, added last 20 min)
- Whole tinned San Marzano tomatoes, smashed
- Yellow potatoes
- Onion, garlic, olive oil, bay leaf, parsley
