Made this with Nat, mostly following the America's Test Kitchen version. We kept their move of mincing the anchovies straight into the dressing instead of draping fillets on top, and used their 2 parts lemon to 3 parts olive oil ratio for the dressing.

For the tuna we used Tonnino oil-packed, the espelette pepper variety. We served everything over a bed of arugula, which was also mostly what we dressed.

We skipped the grape tomatoes and capers, and used plain green olives instead of niçoise. The eggs were boiled 8 minutes for a jammy yolk. We didn't crisp the potatoes — just boiled them in salted water — and boiled the green beans in very salted water too.

The baby potatoes took about 30 minutes to cook through, not the 12–15 the recipe says.

## Ingredients
- Tonnino oil-packed tinned tuna (espelette pepper variety)
- Anchovy fillets, minced into the dressing (in place of draping on top)
- Baby potatoes, boiled in salted water (~30 min, not 12–15)
- Green beans, boiled in very salted water
- Eggs, boiled 8 min (jammy)
- Green olives (in place of niçoise olives)
- Arugula (the bed, and what got dressed)
- Dressing: lemon + olive oil at 2:3, Dijon, minced shallot, minced anchovy
- Skipped: grape tomatoes, capers
