Been hunting fava beans for about two months — nobody had them — and finally found them at Rainbow Grocery, so this was a while coming. Based it on Adrianna Adarme's fava mash ricotta toasts but changed a few things.

Used fresh goat cheese instead of ricotta, added lemon zest, and didn't bother mashing the beans — just piled them whole on top. The toast was warm enough to soften the goat cheese, which made it easy to spread.

Liked it, didn't love it. The beans themselves were a little meh — the lemon zest and mint were doing most of the flavor work. Even with very salty blanching water the beans stayed flat and I kept adding salt at every step. They were also hard to mash and kept sliding around the bowl.

## Ingredients

- Fresh fava beans, double-shelled
- Fresh goat cheese (in place of ricotta)
- Fresh mint
- Lemon — juice and zest (added zest)
- Olive oil
- Salt
- Toasted bread

## Next time

- Add a salty, umami pecorino for depth
- Still do the goat cheese, but whip it so it's less dense
