Made this as the centerpiece of a Greek-leaning dinner for four, alongside new potatoes with herb vinaigrette and whipped ricotta with roasted grapes.

I was looking for a recipe that first covers the leg of lamb in Dijon mustard before the herbs go on — the mustard sets the herb crust in a way I really like. There's a Raymond Blanc recipe for Provençal Best End of Spring Lamb that does this for a rack of lamb and it's so much nicer than just the standard garlic and herbs. Still didn't quite nail the oven temp and timing though — was testing frequently but it still went over the medium rare guidance.

## Ingredients
- Boneless leg of lamb (~2.5 lbs)
- Dijon mustard (enough to coat generously)
- Fresh rosemary, thyme, oregano
- Garlic
- Olive oil
- Fleur de sel, black pepper
